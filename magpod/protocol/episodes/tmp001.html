The (First) Report

Case File: CAT1RBC5257-12052022-09012024

Report Giver/Subject: Harriet Winstead

Report Reader: NORRIS

Report Appearances: Arthur, ‘Arthur’, Darla Winstead

Report Locations: Grantham Cemetery

Report Summary:

This report follows an email from Harriet (hpw332@mailpod.com) to someone named ‘Darla’ (dwinny@mailpod.com), who can be assumed to be her family member due to them sharing surnames. Harriet had been speaking with a consultant (most likely a grief consultant), whose name was never mentioned, as she was mourning the loss of Arthur, her late partner of 20 years. The consultant told Harriet to go to Grantham Cemetery over the phone one night; She mentions how he sounded excited in an off-putting way on that phone call, which was unlike him. At the cemetery, instead of the consultant, she finds ‘Arthur’; A discolored, mismatched, undead version of Arthur. Despite this, it sounded just like Arthur, and it recognized Harriet. When asked, it responded that it was ‘some of’ Arthur. She runs, and doesn’t get followed. Harriet asks Darla if she’s free, as she’s afraid of staying at her home due to her newfound fear of Arthur.

Entity Influence:

Primarily The End; seen in the death, graveyard/cemetery, and [partial] reanimation of Arthur. Potentially mixed with The Stranger; hints of it are within the suddenly off-putting, uncanny behavior of the consultant, ‘Arthur’s features being mismatched, mentions of his shape being ‘wrong’, something having potentially been pressing against his skin from the inside, and ‘Arthur’ saying “Some of him.” in response to Harriet. This is high speculation.

Follow-up Summary:

Sam was planning on filing it under ‘Zombies,’ to which Alice was going to let him do, as she believes nobody reads the reports they file. Gwen steps in and suggests, instead, to file it under ‘Reanimation, partial’ and cross-link it with ‘regret.’ She also adds that it could fall under ‘Amalgamative, semi.’ Sam goes with Gwen’s first suggestion, which Alice doesn’t exactly like.

Of Significance:

Nothing of particular significance has been noted as of yet in this report.

The (Second) Report

Case File: CAT23RAB2155-10042022-09012024

Report Giver/Subject: RedCanary

Report Reader: CHESTER

Report Appearances: ArcherK, BadGrav31, Devan, FlowersUnderground

Report Locations: forums.lostcityurbex.com, The Magnus Institute (Ruins)

Report Artifacts: Old wooden box with strange symbols

Report Summary:

This one follows an online forum (forums.lostcityurbex.com) for spelunking and old sites. A user going by RedCanary started a new topic on The Magnus Institute ruins, asking about its status; It’s said to be fully explored, however there’s no online information on the site, thus RedCanary wants to know if exploring it is worth it. Another user, BadGrav31, says to go for it, as the lack of information online is good enough reason to check it out oneself. A moderator for the forum, ArcherK, says it was on the ‘cleared’ list as that’s what one of Devan's (whom I assume is a former forum admin/mod) old lists had it placed under; ArcherK assumes this was a mistake on Devan’s part. RedCanary decides they’ll check it out, and they’ll report their findings on their return.

Once they return from their exploration, RedCanary makes a post where they say it’s definitely not cleared and that they’ll make their full report the following day. A user named FlowersUnderground joins the topic thread, asking about pictures of the ruins, to which RedCanary says they’re having trouble with uploading the files due to photographic distortion. They also mention their increasing paranoia.

They detail some of their findings, one of which being some odd graffiti, most being strange symbols; RedCanary mentions the symbols potentially being of the occult. BadGrav31 expresses curiosity about these graffiti, and asks if it’s possible to get pictures. RedCanary responds that the photos from their exploration are more than likely gone, due to the corrupted files; they then mention a strange, wooden box, they found, as well. The box contains some of the aforementioned symbols; Though, they couldn’t post images of this as well, due to the same photographic distortion issues. They once again mention their increasing paranoia. ArcherK says to RedCanary that the forum does not endorse theft from exploration sites, and that RedCanary better not be admitting to taking souvenirs. RedCanary lashes out, saying they don’t need anonymous DMs, which ArcherK explicitly mentions the forum doesn’t allow, calling them a thief; They also threaten that they know how to dox people, too, if needed. This sudden aggression is likely attributed to RedCanary’s growing paranoia — this outburst does, however, lead to RedCanary getting a formal warning for breaking the rules.

A week later, BadGrav31 posts in the forum topic that they’re still waiting on the graffiti pictures if they’re retrievable. There’s a post from RedCanary with a now-removed image of somebody's eyes, potentially after being gouged out, with the caption: “Canaries should stay above ground.”

Entity Influence:

Primarily, and seemingly only, The Eye; The main focus of the episode, The Magnus Institute, itself having been built in reverence to Beholding, RedCanary’s constant paranoia after the excursion, RedCanary pursuing the knowledge to their [implied] demise.

Follow-up Summary:

Sam asks Gwen if she’s ever heard of The Magnus Institute, to which she says no, and asks him why he wants to know. He says it’s nothing, and that it’s simply a blast from the past.

Of Significance:

The first look into The Magnus Protocol’s Magnus Institute, which is known to have burned down in 1999. There’s not much else known of it as of yet, aside from the descriptions RedCanary gave, as well as the things revealed within the ARG.

RedCanary is likely a reference to canaries in coal mines; you send them in before going into a cave to mine, knowing not to go in due to CO2 poisoning if the canary does not fly out of the cave or dies soon after flying out. They are an explicit warning to Sam, warning him not to look further into the Institute. He, of course, will not listen to this warning.
